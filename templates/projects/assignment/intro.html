Assigning students to courses

Problem

How can we assign first-year students to courses, given their first four preferences?

Parameters and Variables

Parameters:

  • Students \( S_{i} \): \( 1 \leq i \leq n \)
  • Courses \( C_{j} \): \( 1 \leq j \leq m \)
  • Preferences \( P_{i} \): The first four preferred course of each \( S_{i} \)
  • \( X_{j} \): set of students i having \( C_{j} \) as one of their choices where \( j \in P_{i} \)

Decision variables:

\( x_{ijd} \in {0,1} \)

\( x_{ijd} = 1 \) for each pair \( (S_{i},C_{j}) \) where \( j \in P_{i},S_{i} \) is assigned to \( C_{j} \), otherwise \( x_{ijd} = 0 \)

Formulation

Objective

Let \( \alpha \) be the weight of placing a student in a course that is further than the first choice.

Maximize the students' first preferences, in other words, minimize the objective function so most of the students will end up in their first-ranked choice.

\[ \text{min } \sum_{i=1}^n x_{ij_{i,1}} + \alpha x_{ij_{i,2}} + \alpha^2 x_{ij_{i,3}} + \alpha^3 x_{ij_{i,4}}\]

where \( j_{i,1}, ..., j_{i,4} \) are the student S_{i}'s four preferences and \( \alpha > 1 \)

Constraints

  • All students need to be assigned to one of the courses that is in their top four choices

    \[ \sum_{j \in P_{i}} x_{i,j} = 1 \text{, } \forall{i} \leq n \]

  • Course Size Constraint:
    • Let \( (L,U) \) be the minimum and maximum number of students for each course

      • \[ L \leq \sum_{i \in X_{j}}x_{i,j} \leq U \text{, } \forall{j} \leq m \]

    • Then, we introudce \( q_{j,s} \), where s is the size of \( C_{j} \). To ensure each course only takes one capacity, we have:

      • \[ \sum_{s=L}^U s q_{j,s} = 1 \text{, } \forall{j} \]

    • Since courses doesn't have to be perfectly full, we have:

      • \[ \sum_{i \in X_{j}}x_{i,j} \leq \sum_{s=L}^U s q_{j,s} \text{, } \forall{j} \]
